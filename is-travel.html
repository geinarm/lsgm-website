Flying to Iceland

We would recommend booking your tickets through Iceland Air or Wow Air. Iceland Air is amazing and is never a bad choice. If you book early (like yesterday) you can get a good price and they give you two free checked bags. (You know you want to go outlet shopping when you are here!) If you are booking your trip a little later Wow Air might be the good choice. It can be cheaper because it is a no frills airline but you have to be aware of what you are and are not getting.

Travel to Ísafjörður

To get from Reykjavík to Ísafjörður there are two options, a 5 hour drive through the country side, or a 40 minute flight over the mountains.

For domestic flights in Iceland you only have one option, Air Iceland. The flight is quick and easy, there is no security line. Fun fact, the airport in Ísafjörður is apparently one of the most challenging in the world.

If you want to drive you have all the normal rental car options, I like to use rentalcars.com to find a car. Make sure to get the car at the right airport, international flights land in Keflavík (KEF).
Keep in mind that the roads in Iceland do not compare to the highways in the US, especially as you get further away from the capital. There should not be any snow in July, and we do have pavement all the way (that's a recent development).
